# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Primary customers**: Vietnamese consumers from roughly 20 to 50 years old who buy fresh produce for daily meals. They may shop on mobile while moving through a market or supermarket, then use desktop at home to compare products, check orders, and manage account details.

**Secondary customers**: Health-conscious families, office workers, students living away from home, and older users who want clear freshness signals without needing technical knowledge.

**Administrators**: Store operators who manage products, users, stock, and order status. They need a calm operational dashboard that makes pending orders, product quantities, and customer requests easy to scan.

**Usage context**:

- A shopper quickly scans produce from a phone camera before deciding whether to buy.
- A home user browses verified products, checks ratings and freshness cues, then checks out.
- A returning customer updates delivery information and edits or cancels an order before admin confirmation.
- An admin monitors daily orders and changes status from pending to confirmed, shipped, delivered, or cancelled.

**Job to be done**: Help me trust that the food I am buying is fresh, safe, and worth the price, then let me order and track it with minimal friction.

## Product Purpose

FreshFood AI is a fresh-food marketplace with an AI freshness scanner. The product exists to make trust visible: users should not have to rely only on marketing claims, labels, or seller reputation. They can scan, understand, buy, and track fresh products in one flow.

Core capabilities:

- AI freshness detection by camera or uploaded image.
- Product browsing with categories, sorting, ratings, and detail views.
- Cart and checkout for fresh produce orders.
- Customer account page for profile updates, order review, order editing, and cancellation before admin confirmation.
- Admin dashboard for users, products, orders, revenue, stock risk, and order status control.

Success looks like:

- Customers understand freshness results quickly and trust the recommendation.
- Customers can complete checkout without asking for help.
- Pending orders are easy to edit or cancel before confirmation.
- Admins can identify which orders need action without reading every field.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: Trustworthy, Fresh, Practical.

**Voice and tone**:

- Vietnamese-first, direct, warm, and clear.
- Expert about AI and food safety, but never technical for its own sake.
- Confident without hype. Explain the result and the next action.
- Helpful in operational screens. Admin pages should feel focused, not promotional.

**Emotional goals**:

- Calm confidence: the user knows what is fresh and what to do next.
- Everyday usefulness: the app feels like a tool people can use repeatedly.
- Subtle modernity: AI feels accessible, not intimidating.

## Anti-references

FreshFood AI should not look or feel like:

- A generic marketplace clone with crowded banners, noisy badges, and discount-first hierarchy.
- A clinical food safety dashboard that makes shopping feel alarming.
- An AI demo full of model jargon, charts, and unexplained scores.
- A loud startup landing page with decorative gradients and vague claims.
- A flat admin template where every table, card, and button has the same weight.
- A green-only interface where every surface competes with the primary action.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through visible evidence**: Show product names, quantities, order status, freshness result, and clear next actions. Do not hide operational detail when it helps confidence.
2. **Scanner first, marketplace second**: The AI scanner is the differentiator. The shop and account flows should support that trust instead of behaving like a generic catalog.
3. **Fast scanning for repeated tasks**: Customers and admins should understand lists at a glance through STT, status labels, product summaries, and consistent table structure.
4. **Edit before commitment**: A pending order is still flexible. Once admin confirms it, the UI should clearly signal that the order is locked.
5. **Quiet polish over decoration**: Use spacing, type, iconography, and restrained color to create quality. Avoid unnecessary cards, gradients, or visual effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA is the minimum target for contrast, keyboard focus, form labels, and status messaging.
- Vietnamese is the primary language. UI text must be encoded correctly and written naturally.
- Mobile-first behavior matters because scanner and shopping flows often happen on phones.
- Admin dashboard must remain usable on desktop with wide tables and dense information.
- Status must never rely on color alone. Pair color with labels, icons, or clear text.
- Motion should be subtle and should respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Touch targets should stay comfortable for older users and mobile users.
